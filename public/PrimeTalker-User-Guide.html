2 Creating an Account

To use PrimeTalker, you need to create a free account. Here's how:

  1. Go to the PrimeTalker landing page and click "Get Started" or "Sign In"
  2. Click "Don't have an account? Sign up" at the bottom
  3. Fill in your details:
    • Username ‚Äî Choose a display name
    • Email ‚Äî Your email address
    • Country ‚Äî Select from dropdown
    • State/Province ‚Äî Select your region
    • Password ‚Äî At least 6 characters
    • Confirm Password ‚Äî Re-enter password
  4. Click "Sign Up"
  5. Check your email for a verification link and click it to confirm your account
üí° Tip: Use a strong password with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols for better security.

3 Signing In

Once you have an account, signing in is quick and easy:

  1. Go to the PrimeTalker landing page
  2. Click "Sign In"
  3. Enter your Email and Password
  4. Click "Sign In" to access your dashboard

Forgot Your Password?

  1. On the sign-in page, click "Forgot password?"
  2. Enter your registered email address
  3. Click "Send Reset Link"
  4. Check your email and click the reset link
  5. Enter your new password twice and save

4 Creating a Room

Start a new meeting room to invite others:

  1. After signing in, you'll see the Rooms page
  2. Under "Create Room", select Your Language from the dropdown
  3. Click "Create New Room"
  4. Your room will be created with a unique Room ID
  5. Copy the Share Link and send it to the person you want to talk to
  6. Click "Enter Room" to join your meeting
‚ö†Ô∏è Note: The share link allows anyone to join your room directly. Share it only with people you trust.

5 Joining a Room

There are two ways to join an existing meeting:

Option A: Using a Share Link

  1. Click the share link someone sent you
  2. If not signed in, log in first
  3. Select Your Language
  4. Click "Join Room"

Option B: Using the Room ID

  1. Go to the Rooms page
  2. Under "Join Room", enter the Room ID
  3. Select Your Language
  4. Click "Join Room"

When you speak:

  1. Your voice is captured and transcribed in real-time
  2. The transcript appears in the side panel

  4. They can respond, and you'll hear their translated response
